Chapter One

A Luling Landmark.

Tucked inconspicuously in the back right-hand corner of the building sits a glass-enclosed smoke room — the inner sanctum of Central Texas barbecue.

Since 1958, pitmasters at City Market have been cooking brisket, sausage, and pork ribs over post oak the slow way. No gas. No shortcuts. The knotty-pine walls and tile floor haven't changed much in sixty-seven years — and neither has the brisket.

Folks fly in from across the country. They drive the fifteen miles south of Lockhart. They wait in line. And they leave with brisket they can cut with a plastic fork.
