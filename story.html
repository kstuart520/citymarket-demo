Our Story

Sixty-Seven Years. Same Smoke.

A Luling, Texas institution established in 1958 — and still cooking the way it always has.

Vintage / exterior / pit room photo
— The Beginning —

It Started in 1958.

In a small Central Texas town fifteen miles south of Lockhart, a smoke pit was lit. Sixty-seven years later, it still hasn't gone out.

City Market opened its doors in Luling in 1958, and from the start it followed one simple rule: real wood, real smoke, no shortcuts. Brisket cooked low over post oak. Sausage hand-stuffed and hand-tied. Sauce made in-house from a recipe that hasn't been tampered with.

The knotty-pine walls went up. The tile floor was laid. The pit was built into the back right corner — a glass-enclosed chamber that still serves as the inner sanctum for pilgrims who come from across the country.

The Legacy

A Timeline of Smoke.

1958

Doors Open

City Market opens in downtown Luling. The original pit is built. First briskets hit the smoke.

1960s

Local Legend

Regulars from Luling, Lockhart, and San Antonio start making the pilgrimage. The sauce recipe gets locked in.

1970s–80s

The BBQ Trail

City Market becomes a required stop on the Central Texas BBQ tour, alongside Kreuz, Smitty's, and Black's in Lockhart.

1990s–2000s

Texas Monthly Top 10

City Market earns a permanent spot in the Texas Monthly BBQ rankings. The Today Show comes to film.

2015

Global Recognition

Newsweek names City Market one of the 101 Best Places to Eat in the world — alongside Husk, Daniel, and Momofuku.

Today

Still Smoking

The pit still runs on post oak. The sauce recipe hasn't changed. The line still wraps around the counter by lunchtime.
